Why Radar

Every day, organizations experience privacy and security incidents involving regulated data. Both privacy and security teams are not only challenged to keep up with ever-changing global data breach notification laws, but are required to respond quickly and accurately in order to meet their regulatory obligations. With a maze of increasingly complex global regulations - including New York Department of Financial Services (NYDFS), California Consumer Protection Act (CCPA), and EUâ€™s General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) - the need to respond efficiently and within mandated timelines is paramount to reducing risk and eliminating the exposure to potential fines.

With the Splunk Phantom Radar integration, Radar enables the efficient communication between security and privacy teams allowing a timely handoff of critical information while staying compliant, thus reducing the overall Mean Time To Privacy Risk Assessment (MTTPRA). When security investigates a case and learns that the incident involves regulated data, security, using manual actions or Playbooks, can send incident information to their privacy counterparts via the Radar SaaS platform.

With the advent of a mandatory reporting window of just 72 hours with GDPR, regardless of a completed security investigation, the early involvement of privacy leads to increased efficiency and the ability to meet aggressive notification deadlines.

Incident Response Automation

Automation in incident response eliminates the subjectivity and inconsistency that is inherent in manual approaches. Radar provides a consistent approach to privacy incident response that promotes clear communication between the security and privacy teams and accelerates the decision-making process. This in turn eliminates the risk of over- and under-notification and helps your organization reduce risk, save time and cost, and build trust.

Asset Configuration

Configuration that must be set are under the "Asset Settings" tab. Below lists some details for each configuration setting:

  • Radar API Bearer Token - A valid Radar bearer token with privileges for reading and writing radar incidents and notes.
  • Radar API URL - defaults to the production URL, but can be set to a test or staging URL.
  • Incident Group ID - must be set to the incident group of the user associated with the bearer token.
  • Time Zone - The timezone to be associated with the asset. Used to set the Radar incident 'discovered' time.

Environment Variables

Under the 'Advanced' section of the asset settings, set environment variables to configure the app settings:

ALLOW_SELF_SIGNED_CERTS - Should be set to '1' to turn off verification of SSL Certificates during action requests.
